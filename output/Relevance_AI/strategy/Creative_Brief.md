# Creative Brief: Relevance AI

## Creative Direction

Relevance AI should present as technically advanced but operationally practical. The visual system needs to communicate control, speed, and implementation confidence at first glance. Use high-contrast hierarchy, restrained motion cues, and decisive CTA treatment so creative work reads like an execution engine rather than a generic innovation brand.

## Design Principles

- Prioritize outcome clarity over decorative novelty in every hero frame.
- Use contrast to separate authority statements from CTA moments.
- Keep typography disciplined: headline for decision framing, body for implementation proof.
- Design every asset as part of a sequence, not a standalone poster.
- Ensure visual cadence supports scanning behavior on mobile and LinkedIn feed contexts.
- Maintain a consistent anchor motif so multi-day assets feel like one operating system.
- Prevent ambiguous symbolism that could dilute technical credibility.

## Visual Motifs

### Motif 1: Signal Lanes
- **Description:** Linear bands and directional overlays that suggest workflow progression.
- **Why it fits the brand:** Matches Minimalist operator-grade posture and reinforces execution momentum.
- **Where to use it:** Carousel openers, transition slides, and demo CTA panels.
- **What to avoid:** Overly decorative paths that distract from headline hierarchy.

### Motif 2: Control Grid
- **Description:** Structured modular grid informed by patterns such as Whitespace-led layout, Hierarchy-driven blocks.
- **Why it fits the brand:** Communicates technical rigor and controlled complexity.
- **Where to use it:** LinkedIn carousel value slides and dashboard-like stat frames.
- **What to avoid:** Crowded matrices with no visual anchor for the CTA.

### Motif 3: Mission Spotlight
- **Description:** High-contrast focus zone that reinforces mission anchor: Building the world's autonomous AI workforce..
- **Why it fits the brand:** Keeps narrative coherence across channels and days.
- **Where to use it:** Day 1 hooks, Day 5 conversion push, final reinforcement assets.
- **What to avoid:** Generic hero compositions that could belong to any AI vendor.

## Artifact Guidance

### linkedin carousel artifact
- **Format:** Multi-frame sequential narrative
- **Layout direction:** Hook-first visual hierarchy with rapid scan blocks
- **Content hierarchy:** Headline claim, supporting proof, objection release, then conversion move.
- **CTA treatment:** Keep a single CTA expression (Build your AI workforce) with accent-color emphasis and no competing secondary action.
- **Visual risk to avoid:** Do not let visual novelty outrun buyer clarity; if a frame does not advance decision confidence, remove it.

## Image / Background Prompt Rules

- Anchor prompts in operational outcomes, not abstract futurism.
- Require abstract, text-free backgrounds for generated motif assets.
- Mention palette intent explicitly (primary for authority, accent for CTA).
- Reference visual tone and design patterns from validated brand signals before generation.
- Reject prompts that imply generic AI iconography, stock-human scenes, or novelty-for-novelty gradients.

## Do Not Do

- Do not use vague “future of AI” language without an operator outcome attached.
- Do not overload slides with parallel CTAs that compete for attention.
- Do not introduce unrelated illustration styles that break visual continuity.
- Do not flatten hierarchy by giving proof points and headlines equal visual weight.
- Do not publish artifacts where accent color dominates non-conversion surfaces.
