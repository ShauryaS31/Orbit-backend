# Heidi LinkedIn Style Playbook

## 1. Brand Summary

Heidi is a clinical workflow AI partner for healthcare teams. On LinkedIn, Heidi should read as **calm, institutional, and proof-led**—never hypey, never sci-fi, never generic SaaS. Posts and images feel like **editorial healthcare cards**: bright pale yellow and cream surfaces, deep burgundy ink typography, and short, trustworthy phrases about documentation burden, capacity returned to care, and partnerships.

## 2. Visual Identity

- **Editorial healthcare brand system**—think hospital annual report meets modern product design, not consumer app gradients.
- **Square-native LinkedIn cards** (1:1) with generous margins and clear hierarchy.
- **Bright pale / lemon yellow fields** aligned with real Heidi LinkedIn creative, balanced with **cream and off-white** panels.
- **Minimal abstract loop motif** only when subtle, geometric, and non-logo-like—never a fake crest or seal.

## 3. Color Palette

- **Primary fields:** pale lemon / butter yellow (`#FFF86B`–`#FFF9E6` range).
- **Surfaces:** cream, warm white, soft paper (`#FCFAF8`, `#FFF8EE`).
- **Type / ink:** deep burgundy, dark brown, near-black (`#4A1622`, `#28030F`).
- **Accents:** restrained; clinical blue only when implying partner neutrality—never dominant purple or neon.

## 4. Typography Direction

- **Headlines:** clean serif, confident, short—often two lines max on-card.
- **Supporting copy:** smaller humanist sans or restrained serif—never dense legal text in the image.
- **Labels:** small sans pills (“Partnership”, “Proof”) when needed—never long sentences.

## 5. Common Post Formats

- **Report-stat / proof tile**—yellow field + proof line; one idea per card.
- **Partnership announcement**—cream or split panel; institutional, restrained.
- **Clinician truth / burnout truth**—direct statement on documentation load or capacity.
- **Brand statement**—single thesis; minimal motif.
- **Conference / session**—agenda clarity without stock photo clutter.

## 6. Image Composition Rules

- **1080×1080** export mindset; keep all meaningful content inside safe margins (~8%).
- **No long paragraphs** in the raster—**prefer 3–8 words** of visible on-image text.
- **One focal headline** + optional short label + optional footer “Heidi Health” or “Heidi”.
- **Leave breathing room**; if text rendering is uncertain, favor clean blocks of color and empty space for overlay.
- **No fake logos**; **no fake partner marks**; no photorealistic “logo” text.

## 7. Copy Voice

- Clinical, direct, calm.
- Name tensions: documentation, after-hours work, capacity, patient-facing time.
- Prefer **grounded outcomes** over superlatives.
- Captions may be longer; **images stay short**.

## 8. Proof / Claim Rules

- **Do not invent statistics.** No fabricated percentages, hours, or minutes.
- Only use numeric claims when **explicitly sourced** in approved workflow evidence.
- Prefer qualitative proof: “Less after-hours paperwork”, “More time with patients”.

## 9. What To Avoid

- Parchment, certificate borders, wax seals, ribbons, ornate leaves.
- Stock doctors shaking hands, generic hospital stock.
- Sci-fi, robots, holographic brains, “AI magic” sparkles.
- **Relevance-style** purple / pixel / arcade / neon gamer language in visuals or on-image copy.
- Long paragraph blocks inside the image.
- Cropped or unreadable type.
- Fake health-system or partner logos.

## 10. Good Visual Directions

- “Split panel: yellow proof field + cream narrative field.”
- “Large serif headline over soft yellow; single proof line in smaller type.”
- “Partnership strip with restrained typography—no logos.”
- “Minimal loop motif as tiny abstract mark—low contrast.”

## 11. Bad Visual Directions

- “Dashboard screenshot with metrics.”
- “Glowing neural network brain.”
- “75% of clinicians…” on-image (unsupported stat).
- “Boost productivity” in giant display type.
- Dense paragraph text filling the card.

## 12. Image Prompt Contract

When generating a Heidi LinkedIn image, the model **must**:

1. Treat this playbook as the **source of truth** for palette, composition, and tone.
2. Produce a **square 1080×1080** composition suitable for LinkedIn (1:1).
3. Use **only** the **provided visible text strings** for any readable words—no extra marketing sentences.
4. **Never** invent statistics or time-savings numbers in visible type.
5. **Never** draw fake logos or partner marks.
6. Avoid forbidden motifs listed in section 9.
7. Prefer **editorial card** layouts over photo collages.

## 13. Caption Contract

- Captions may expand on the idea; they should stay **clinical and proof-oriented**.
- Do not stack hype adjectives; avoid “AI-powered transformation” boilerplate.
- Ground claims; do not invent metrics in captions either.

## 14. Example Post Concepts

- **Documentation steals time from care** — yellow field, burgundy headline, one supporting line.
- **Partnership built for calmer care** — cream panel, partnership label, no logos.
- **Capacity returned to care** — stat-card rhythm without fake numbers unless sourced.
- **Your AI care partner** — minimal brand statement, small abstract loop optional.

### Approved short on-image text examples

- “Documentation steals time from care”
- “More time with patients”
- “Less after-hours paperwork”
- “Capacity returned to care”
- “Your AI care partner”
- “Partnership”
- “Relief on the Record”

### Bad on-image text examples

- “75% of clinicians…”
- “Clinicians save 5 hours…”
- “Boost productivity”
- “AI-powered transformation”
- Any long paragraph or cropped ellipsis-heavy line
